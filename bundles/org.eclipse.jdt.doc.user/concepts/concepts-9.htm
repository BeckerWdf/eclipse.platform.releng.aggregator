Refactoring support

The goal of refactoring is to improve source code without changing its behavior. Refactoring means behavior-preserving program transformation. When you refactor your code, your goal is to make a system-wide code change without affecting the behavior of the system. The JDT helps you automatically manage refactorings.

The JDT supports a number of transformations described in Martin Fowler's book Refactoring: Improving the Design of Existing Code, Addison Wesley 1999.

When performing a refactoring operation, you can optionally preview all the impending changes resulting from a refactoring action before you finally choose to carry them out. Thus, you can execute a refactoring in one of two different ways:

  • Refactoring without preview
    In a quick refactoring, you skip being notified of potential problems and the refactoring preview. If a Stop problem is detected, the refactoring will not be carried out.
  • Refactoring with preview
    In a controlled refactoring, you are notified of potential problems and are given a detailed preview of what the refactoring action will do.

Refactoring commands are available from the context menus in may views and editors.
